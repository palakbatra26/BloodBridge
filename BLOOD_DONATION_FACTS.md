## Life-Saving Impact

### Every Drop Counts
- **1 donation** can save up to **3 lives**
- Every **2 seconds**, someone needs blood
- **36,000 units** of red blood cells are needed daily in the US
- **7,000 units** of platelets are needed daily

### Global Need
- Only **3%** of eligible people donate blood annually
- **1 in 7** people entering a hospital needs blood
- **Blood cannot be manufactured** - it can only come from donors
- A single car accident victim can require up to **100 units** of blood

## Blood Types & Compatibility

### Universal Donors & Recipients
- **O-negative**: Universal red cell donor (can donate to anyone)
- **AB-positive**: Universal plasma donor
- **O-positive**: Most common blood type (37% of population)
- **AB-negative**: Rarest blood type (less than 1% of population)

### Blood Type Distribution
- O-positive: 37%
- O-negative: 7%
- A-positive: 36%
- A-negative: 6%
- B-positive: 8%
- B-negative: 2%
- AB-positive: 3%
- AB-negative: 1%

## Health Benefits for Donors

### Physical Benefits
- **Reduces iron overload**: Helps prevent hemochromatosis
- **Burns calories**: Donating blood burns approximately 650 calories
- **Free health screening**: Get a mini-physical with every donation
- **Reduces heart disease risk**: Regular donation may lower cardiovascular risk
- **Stimulates blood cell production**: Body replenishes donated blood

### Mental Benefits
- **Sense of purpose**: Knowing you saved lives
- **Reduced stress**: Helping others releases endorphins
- **Community connection**: Part of a life-saving network

## Donation Process

### Time Investment
- **Registration**: 5-10 minutes
- **Health screening**: 10-15 minutes
- **Donation**: 8-10 minutes
- **Refreshments**: 10-15 minutes
- **Total time**: 30-45 minutes

### What You Donate
- **Whole blood**: 1 pint (450ml)
- **Platelets**: 1-2 hours (can donate every 7 days)
- **Plasma**: 1 hour (can donate every 28 days)
- **Double red cells**: 30 minutes (can donate every 112 days)

## Recovery & Replenishment

### How Fast Your Body Recovers
- **Plasma**: Replenished within 24 hours
- **Red blood cells**: Fully replaced in 4-6 weeks
- **Platelets**: Replenished within 72 hours
- **White blood cells**: Restored within days

### Donation Frequency
- **Whole blood**: Every 56 days (8 weeks)
- **Platelets**: Every 7 days (up to 24 times/year)
- **Plasma**: Every 28 days
- **Double red cells**: Every 112 days (16 weeks)

## Who Needs Blood?

### Medical Conditions
- **Cancer patients**: Chemotherapy affects blood cell production
- **Surgery patients**: Major surgeries require blood transfusions
- **Trauma victims**: Accidents and emergencies
- **Chronic illnesses**: Sickle cell disease, thalassemia
- **Burn victims**: Extensive burns require plasma
- **Organ transplants**: Require multiple blood products
- **Premature babies**: Often need transfusions

### Emergency Situations
- Natural disasters
- Mass casualty events
- Terrorist attacks
- Industrial accidents
- War zones

## Eligibility Requirements

### Basic Requirements
- **Age**: 18-65 years (varies by country)
- **Weight**: Minimum 50 kg (110 lbs)
- **Health**: Generally good health
- **Hemoglobin**: Minimum 12.5 g/dL
- **Blood pressure**: Within normal range
- **Pulse**: 50-100 beats per minute

### Temporary Deferrals
- Recent tattoo or piercing: 3-12 months
- Recent travel to malaria zones: 1-3 years
- Recent vaccination: 1-4 weeks
- Pregnancy: 6 weeks after delivery
- Recent surgery: 6-12 months
- Antibiotics: 1-2 weeks after completion

## Myths vs Facts

### Myth: Donating blood is painful
**Fact**: You may feel a slight pinch, but most donors report minimal discomfort

### Myth: Donating makes you weak
**Fact**: Your body replaces the fluid within 24 hours. You may feel slightly tired but can resume normal activities

### Myth: You can get diseases from donating
**Fact**: Impossible! All equipment is sterile and used only once

### Myth: Older people can't donate
**Fact**: Healthy individuals up to 65+ can donate with doctor approval

### Myth: Vegetarians can't donate
**Fact**: Vegetarians can donate if they meet hemoglobin requirements
